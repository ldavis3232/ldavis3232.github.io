Literature Review & Hypothesis

There is an abundance of scholarly research on political orientation in relation to socio-economic traits. There is larger amount of scholarly research on racial attitudes  in relation to  socio-economic traits. A number of  studies have attempted to discover the link between social status or “class” and political attitudes . My first 3 articles reference below investigate this link in various ways for example,  “Status Consistency and Political Attitudes” is centered around a class theory  “Status Consistency”  and it ability to predict political attitudes such as political orientation. In contrast, “Impact of class on political attitudes” is a testing a theory but analytically investigating the link between class and political attitude through survey analysis.   After reviewing a number of scholarly works on political orientation and socio-economic traits, I hypothesize that socio-economic traits do strongly influence political orientation. In regards to my second topic, I hypothesize that socio-economic traits do strongly influence racial attitudes.
